Tropical depression outside PAR intensifies into Tropical
Storm Fung-wong

Author: Acor Arceo, 3:43 pm

MANILA, Philippines – The tropical depression outside the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR) strengthened into a tropical storm at 2 am on Thursday, November 6, and was given the international name Fung-wong.

Fung-wong, which means “phoenix,” is a name contributed by Hong Kong.

The tropical storm was last spotted 1,715 kilometers east of northeastern Mindanao as of 4 am on Thursday, moving northwest at 20 kilometers per hour (km/h).

The Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) expects Fung-wong to rapidly intensify into a severe tropical storm within 24 hours; a typhoon on Friday, November 7; and a super typhoon on Saturday, November 8.

It is expected to enter PAR as a typhoon late Friday evening or early Saturday morning, and would be given the local name Uwan.

PAGASA maintained that while there is still high uncertainty in the forecast, there appears to be “an increasing chance of landfall” in Northern Luzon or Central Luzon on Monday, November 10.

The weather bureau may raise tropical cyclone wind signals in the eastern part of Luzon and portions of Samar Island as early as Friday evening or Saturday morning to give these areas time to prepare for fierce winds.

If the future Uwan becomes a super typhoon, Signal No. 5 would be the highest possible wind signal.

PAGASA warned the public that weather conditions may start to deteriorate on Sunday, November 9. By Monday and Tuesday, November 11, “potentially life-threatening stormy conditions may occur over Northern Luzon and portions of Central Luzon,” added the weather bureau.

PAGASA may also release storm surge warnings as early as Saturday, with a high coastal flooding risk seen in Northern Luzon and the eastern coast of Central Luzon.

For the country’s seaboards, moderate to rough conditions could begin in the northern and eastern seaboards of Luzon and the eastern seaboards of the Visayas and Mindanao on Friday or Saturday. The western and southern seaboards of Luzon may also have moderate to rough seas by mid-Saturday or on Sunday.

From Sunday onwards, sea conditions will already be hazardous for all vessels in most of Luzon and the eastern seaboard of the Visayas, with waves reaching very rough, high, very high, or “phenomenal” levels.

PAGASA advised the public to keep monitoring updates on the incoming tropical cyclone, especially since forecasts can still change.
